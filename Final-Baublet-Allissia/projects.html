Project Details

BSO

The Bloomington Symphony Orchestra's website was redesigned by Adam Hughes, Alex Flinchum, Emily Wattimena, Deepthi Devireddy, Jesica Courtney and myself. We are part of an Indiana University organization called ServeIT and our mission is to provide nonprofit organizations with new/updated technology that they cannot afford or need assistance with. BSO came to ServeIT looking to redesign their site so that it was modern and expressed their mission and values.

Quote Design

This project focused more on design using typography, positioning, and colors related to an image that would appeal to an audience. The toughest part of this project was determining which part of the quote to emphasize and if that part truly represented the quote and image's focus. I chose the quote, almost at random, and attempted to make something pretty. I had trouble with some positioning of the quote marks and spacing between the words and lines. However, I believe it turned out quite well.

Dagwoods Redesign

For this project, I started by researching multiple websites and assessing their usability to determine which site would benefit most from a new, updated design. I decided on Dagwood's Deli's site, as there was mass disorganization, broken link, confusing layouts, and noisy graphics. I redesigned their site to be responsive, organized, easily usable. I was unable to finish the "about" page as I had ran out of time. That page is also less than responsive since I did not address the responsiveness of each element as I inserted it. I have use that lesson while making my personal site, being sure to make each element responsive as I add it to a page.

Hybrid Animal

This project part of a course focused on Adobe Photoshop and it's functions. The exercise was meant to teach proper selecting and positioning, as well as color blending. We were tasked to choose two animals for our composition and I chose a wolf, because I admire them, as well as a squirrel, because my best friend hates them. With a well composed image, I have created the, uncreatively named, "Wolf-Squirrel".

OSXtern

This project was one completed as a techscreen for Xtern 2018. I was given a prompt for an application called OSXtern that kept the user connected to Indianapolis and TechPoint. After a few days of hard work and feedback, this is the final result (link above). I created the mockup using the beta version of Adobe XD, which is a very useful tool as a starting designer.

Ucommon

This project was used to apply Human Computer Interaction theories and create a mockup of an application made for students. My team and I developed a concept of an app that provides access an online community forum, the ability to apply for jobs and internships, and a virtual network of connections to their peers and professionals. InVision provides the mockup with some functionality for our conceptual idea.
